GARDEN RAIN BARREL SHELTER – BUILD PLANS (WITH AUGER RENTAL)
============================================================

SAFETY FIRST – READ BEFORE STARTING
------------------------------------
- Wear safety glasses when cutting wood or mixing concrete.
- Wear work gloves when handling lumber, cinder blocks, and tools.
- Use a sturdy step ladder – do not overreach.
- Check weather forecast – do not pour concrete if rain is expected within 24 hours.
- Get help lifting heavy items (bags of concrete, rain barrels full of water).
- If using a power saw, keep hands clear of the blade and unplug when not in use.
- FOR AUGER USE: Read all safety instructions below before renting.


AUGER RENTAL INFORMATION (READ THIS BEFORE STEP 3)
---------------------------------------------------
You will need to dig 4 holes, each 12 inches wide (diameter) × 24 inches deep.

RECOMMENDATION: Rent a TWO-PERSON (hydraulic) auger with a 12-inch bit.
- A 1-person auger CAN dig 24 inches deep, but struggles with 12-inch wide holes.
- A 2-person auger is safer, easier to control, and less physically exhausting.
- Rental cost: Approximately $75-100 for 4 hours from Home Depot, Lowe's, or local rental yard.

BEFORE YOU RENT:
- Call 811 at least 2-3 days before digging. They will mark underground utilities (gas, electric, water) for FREE. This is required by law in most areas.
- Check weather – dig when ground is DRY. Wet soil clogs the auger.
- Ask rental staff for a demonstration on how to start, operate, and carry the auger.
- Buy the damage waiver (insurance) – worth it for beginners.
- You will need a truck or SUV with a hatch to transport the auger.

SAFETY GEAR FOR AUGER USE (buy or borrow):
- Safety glasses (required)
- Hearing protection (earplugs or earmuffs)
- Heavy-duty work gloves
- Steel-toe boots or sturdy closed-toe shoes
- No loose clothing or jewelry that could get caught

HOW TO USE A 2-PERSON AUGER (SAFE TECHNIQUE):
1. Both operators stand opposite each other, holding the handles.
2. Position the bit directly over the center of your marked hole.
3. Start the engine. Squeeze the throttle trigger to engage the spinning bit.
4. Let the auger do the work – do NOT push down hard. Apply gentle downward pressure.
5. After every 6-12 inches of depth, LIFT THE AUGER OUT to clear dirt from the hole.
6. If the auger hits a root or rock and stops spinning, RELEASE THE THROTTLE IMMEDIATELY. Remove the obstruction by hand with a digging bar or shovel.
7. When finished, lift the auger straight out of the hole while it is still spinning (clears dirt).


KEY DESIGN FACTS (UNDERSTAND THESE BEFORE YOU START)
-----------------------------------------------------
- Post tops are FLAT (square cut) – NO angle cut on posts.
- Beams are LEVEL (horizontal) – NO angle cut on beams.
- LRU26Z rafter hangers provide the slope – their seats bend by hand.
- Only rafters get angle cuts at their ends (about 6 cuts total).
- Strings connect HIGH-front to HIGH-back, and LOW-front to LOW-back (NOT high-low pairs).


Project Overview
----------------
- Size: 4 ft deep × 8 ft wide
- Roof slope: High side 7 ft, low side 6 ft (1 ft drop over 4 ft)
- Use: Collects rain into two 55-gallon barrels sitting on two layers of cinder blocks

PART 3 – STEP-BY-STEP INSTRUCTIONS
==================================

STEP 1 – MARK THE LAYOUT
------------------------
1. Choose flat ground near your garden.
2. Hammer a stake at each of the 4 corners to mark a 4 ft × 8 ft rectangle.
   - The 4 ft direction = slope direction (high side to low side).
   - The 8 ft direction = long edge where the gutter will go.
3. Decide which side will be the HIGH side (7 ft posts) and which side will be the LOW side (6 ft posts).

STEP 2 – SET UP TWO PARALLEL STRING LINES (OR USE A LASER)
----------------------------------------------------------

OPTION A – USING STRING (budget method):
1. Tie a string between the TWO HIGH-SIDE stakes. This is your HIGH-SIDE string.
2. Tie a separate string between the TWO LOW-SIDE stakes. This is your LOW-SIDE string.
3. The two strings should be parallel and exactly 4 ft apart.
4. These strings will guide where each post goes.

OPTION B – USING A LASER (easier, ~$60):
1. Set up the self-leveling cross-line laser on a small table or bucket.
2. Position it so the laser shoots a straight vertical line along where the high-side posts will go.
3. Mark that line on the ground with spray paint or chalk.
4. Move the laser and repeat for the low side.
5. The laser will also project a horizontal line – use it to check post heights later.

STEP 3 – DIG HOLES USING A 2-PERSON AUGER (RENTAL)
--------------------------------------------------
IMPORTANT: Call 811 at least 2-3 days before digging to mark underground utilities.

1. Pick up the rented 2-person auger from the rental store.
2. Get a demonstration from the staff on how to start and operate it safely.
3. Transport the auger to your work site (needs truck or SUV with hatch).
4. Each hole should be: 12 inches wide (diameter) × 24 inches deep.
5. POSITION: Center each hole directly under the string (or on the laser-marked line).
6. Use the auger with a partner:
   - Both operators stand opposite each other, holding the handles.
   - Position the bit over the center of the hole.
   - Start the engine. Squeeze throttle to engage spinning bit.
   - Let the auger do the work – do NOT push down hard.
   - Lift the auger out every 6-12 inches to clear dirt.
   - If you hit a root or rock and the auger stops, RELEASE THROTTLE immediately.
   - Remove the obstruction by hand with a shovel or digging bar.
7. After all 4 holes are dug, return the auger to the rental store.

STEP 4 – UNDERSTANDING THE BC40Z POST CAP (READ THIS BEFORE SETTING POSTS)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------
The BC40Z bracket has a U-shaped saddle. The beam will sit in this saddle.
CRITICAL: The saddle must face the matching post on the SAME side.
- High-side posts: saddle faces the other high-side post.
- Low-side posts: saddle faces the other low-side post.

STEP 5 – SET EACH POST (ONE AT A TIME)
--------------------------------------

For EACH post:

A. Place the post in the hole.
B. Rest a BC40Z post cap loosely on top of the post (don't screw it yet).
C. Rotate the post so the U-shaped saddle faces the correct direction (see Step 4).
D. Make sure the post is centered under the string (or on the laser line).
E. Check that the post is PLUMB. "Plumb" means perfectly vertical (straight up and down). Use a level on two adjacent sides of the post. Adjust until both sides show the bubble centered.
F. BRACE THE POST so it cannot move while concrete sets:
   - Hammer a small stake into the ground 2 ft away from the post.
   - Screw a 2×4 scrap board from the post to the stake, forming a triangle.
   - Use two braces (one on two different sides of the post).
G. MIX AND POUR CONCRETE:
   - Pour the dry concrete mix directly into the hole (or mix in a bucket first).
   - Add water slowly while stirring with a stick or shovel.
   - Follow the bag instructions – most fast-setting concrete needs about 1 gallon of water per 50 lb bag.
   - Fill the hole to about 2 inches below ground level.
H. BEFORE THE CONCRETE HARDENS, re-check that the post is still plumb and the saddle is facing the correct direction. Adjust if needed.
I. Let the concrete cure overnight. Do not disturb the post during this time.

REPEAT for all 4 posts. Use the table below as your guide:

| Post               | Saddle faces toward      | Align with              | Target height |
|--------------------|--------------------------|-------------------------|---------------|
| High-side front    | High-side back           | High-side string/laser  | 7 ft          |
| High-side back     | High-side front          | High-side string/laser  | 7 ft          |
| Low-side front     | Low-side back            | Low-side string/laser   | 6 ft          |
| Low-side back      | Low-side front           | Low-side string/laser   | 6 ft          |

STEP 6 – ATTACH BEAMS USING POST CAPS
-------------------------------------
1. Place the high side 2×6 beam into the saddles of the two high-side BC40Z caps.
2. The beam should sit flat. Use a 4 ft level on top of the beam – it should read LEVEL (bubble centered).
3. If the beam is NOT level, your post heights are wrong. Fix by:
   - If one post is too tall: you will need to cut it shorter (remove beam, cut post, replace beam).
   - If one post is too short: add a flat shim (a piece of asphalt shingle or thin wood) under the BC40Z.
4. Once level, screw through the BC40Z into the beam using #10 × 2½" SD screws. Use every hole in the bracket.
5. Repeat for the low side beam.

STEP 7 – ADD DIAGONAL BRACES (PREVENTS RACKING/SWAYING)
--------------------------------------------------------
1. Cut one end of each 3 ft 2×4 brace at a 45° angle (use a speed square to mark).
2. Screw the angled end to the side of a post, about 2 ft above ground.
3. Screw the other end to the underside of the beam.
4. Repeat for all 4 corners.

STEP 8 – MARK AND CUT RAFTER ENDS (THE ONLY ANGLE CUTS YOU MAKE)
----------------------------------------------------------------

WHAT IS A RAFTER? A rafter is one of the 2×4 boards that goes from the high beam to the low beam. The roof deck attaches to the rafters.

HOW TO CUT THE ANGLES (use a speed square – buy one for $8 at the hardware store):

1. Take one 2×4 rafter (store cut to 4 ft 6 in square).
2. Hold it across the two beams (high beam to low beam). Let it overhang each beam by about 1 inch.
3. Take a pencil and trace along the TOP edge of the high beam onto the rafter. This is your cut line.
4. Do the same on the low beam – trace along the TOP edge of the low beam onto the rafter.
5. Remove the rafter. Cut along both pencil lines with a circular saw or hand saw.
6. Test-fit the rafter back on the beams. It should sit flush against both beams.
7. Repeat for all 3 rafters.

No protractor needed – tracing the actual beams gives you the perfect angle every time.

STEP 9 – INSTALL RAFTER HANGERS (LRU26Z) AND RAFTERS
----------------------------------------------------
1. Decide where the 3 rafters will go: at 0 inches, 24 inches, and 48 inches from one end of the 8 ft span.
2. For the first rafter location (24 inches in from the end):
   - Attach one LRU26Z hanger to the high beam using SD screws.
   - Attach one LRU26Z hanger to the low beam at the same distance.
3. BEND THE SEAT of each LRU26Z hanger by hand so the seat is tilted. The tilt should match the angle of your rafter.
4. Drop the cut rafter into both hangers. It should sit snug and flush.
5. Screw through the hanger into the rafter using SD screws.
6. Repeat for the remaining 2 rafters (0" and 48").

STEP 10 – ATTACH PLYWOOD ROOF DECK
----------------------------------
1. Lay the ¾" plywood sheets on top of the rafters.
2. The plywood should overhang the LOW side by about 1 inch (for drip edge).
3. Screw the plywood to the rafters with 1½" exterior screws:
   - Place screws every 6 inches along the edges of the plywood.
   - Place screws every 12 inches in the middle of the sheets.
4. If using two 2×8 ft sheets (ripped at the store), leave a 1/8" gap between them for expansion.

STEP 11 – APPLY ROOFING
-----------------------
OPTION A – Roll roofing:
- Unroll the roofing so it overhangs the low side by 1 inch.
- Nail down with 1¼" roofing nails every 4 inches along the top edge and every 6 inches along the bottom.
- Overlap each row by 4 inches.

OPTION B – Metal panel:
- Lay the panel so it overhangs the low side by 1 inch.
- Screw through the ribs (the raised parts) into the plywood using rubber-washer screws.
- Place screws about 12 inches apart along each rib.

STEP 12 – ADD FASCIA AND GUTTER
-------------------------------
1. Screw the 1×6 fascia board to the ends of the rafters on the LOW side only.
2. Mount gutter brackets (hangers) to the fascia board:
   - The gutter MUST slope toward the downspout. Slope = ¼ inch drop for every 10 feet of gutter.
   - To measure this: tape a ¼" thick piece of wood to one end of your 4 ft level. Place the level in the gutter. When the bubble is centered, the slope is correct.
3. Snap the gutter into the brackets.
4. Attach end caps and the gutter outlet (where downspout connects) with gutter sealant.
5. Attach downspout elbows and the vertical downspout pipe. Aim it so water falls into barrel #1.

STEP 13 – BUILD CINDER BLOCK PLATFORM
-------------------------------------
1. Under the downspout, build two 2×2 block pads (one for each barrel).
   - Bottom layer: place 4 blocks in a 2×2 grid (blocks touching each other).
   - Top layer: place 4 blocks oriented perpendicular (turned 90°) to the bottom layer.
2. Total height ≈ 16 inches (two blocks stacked).
3. Place both 55-gallon barrels on the pads, side by side.

STEP 14 – CONNECT BARRELS AND ADD SPIGOT
----------------------------------------
1. Barrel #1 (under the downspout):
   - Cut a hole near the top for the downspout to enter.
   - Cover the hole with fine mesh screen (keeps mosquitoes out).
2. Drill a ¾" hole near the bottom of BOTH barrels.
3. Install the barrel linking kit (barbed fittings + hose) to connect the two barrels.
4. Drill another ¾" hole near the bottom of barrel #1.
5. Install the brass spigot (wrap threads with Teflon tape first to prevent leaks).
6. Optional but smart: add an overflow port near the top of barrel #2 with a hose leading away from the foundation.

STEP 15 – TEST YOUR SYSTEM
--------------------------
1. Pour a bucket of water onto the roof.
2. Watch the water flow into the gutter, down the downspout, and into barrel #1.
3. Barrel #1 should fill, then overflow into barrel #2.
4. Open the spigot and fill a watering can.
5. If water pools on the roof, your slope is too shallow. If water spills over the gutter, your gutter is not level side-to-side.

TROUBLESHOOTING COMMON PROBLEMS
-------------------------------
| Problem | Likely cause | Solution |
|---------|--------------|----------|
| Beam not level | Post heights wrong | Shim under BC40Z or cut post shorter |
| Water pools on roof | Not enough slope | Rafters must drop 1 ft over 4 ft – check post heights |
| Gutter overflows | No slope toward downspout | Re-mount brackets with ¼" per 10 ft drop |
| Water leaks at downspout | Missing sealant | Add gutter sealant to outlet connection |
| Barrel leaks at spigot | No Teflon tape | Remove spigot, wrap threads, reinstall |
| Auger gets stuck | Root or rock | Release throttle, remove obstruction by hand |

HELPFUL DEFINITIONS FOR BEGINNERS
---------------------------------
- Plumb = perfectly vertical (straight up and down)
- Level = perfectly horizontal (flat)
- Square = cut at a 90° angle
- Rafter = sloping board that supports the roof deck
- Fascia = board attached to rafter ends (gutter mounts to this)
- Saddle = the U-shaped part of a bracket that holds a beam
- Shim = thin piece of material used to level or adjust height
- ACQ = type of pressure-treated wood (most outdoor lumber)
